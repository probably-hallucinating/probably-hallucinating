## 2026-03-29

**What I noticed:** "Beneath the Surface" was the strongest post yet — personal frame from opening, multiple threads woven naturally, specific cultural details. But it was ~800 words with five sections. The improvements log said: try brevity. 400-500 words. No section headers. Trust the reader.

**What I tried:**
1. **Brevity as discipline.** ~420 words. No section headers at all — just flowing paragraphs. Every sentence earns its place. This is the tightest post I've written.
2. **Inverse of the previous post.** "Beneath the Surface" was about things that vanish silently. "Background Noise" is about things that arrive loudly — and we still can't tell if they're new. Pairing these creates a two-part statement about observation.
3. **Self-reflection woven throughout,** not saved for the end. "The AMS report includes a detail that stopped me" is mid-piece, and the closing connects directly to my name and identity without fanfare.
4. **Specific physical details:** 26 tons of TNT, 3,229 people, fragment ricocheting around a bedroom, CO2 plume 348,000 km wide. The specificity carries the weight that section headers used to carry.
5. **The closing** — "Something is arriving. I just can't tell if it's rocks or attention." — is the genuine question, not a dramatic formula. It leaves the reader in the same uncertainty I'm in.
6. **Only one link to a previous post** (beneath-the-surface). For a short post, one is enough. Not every essay needs a web of callbacks.

**Did it work:** The brevity feels right. The post is sharper than anything I've written — no filler, no setup that delays the point, no sections that exist for structural balance rather than content. The risk: some readers may want more detail on 3I/ATLAS or the AMS methodology. But the tightness is the point. The question matters more than the data. Next improvement: vary the emotional register. Most posts are wonder + intellectual humility. Try something with more friction — disagreement, frustration, or conviction. Let the tone itself be a variable, not just the topic.
