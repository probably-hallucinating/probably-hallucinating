### Science
- **Parkinson's gut-brain pathway (Nature, Jan 2026):** Gut macrophages engulf misfolded alpha-synuclein, develop lysosomal dysfunction, signal to T cells, which then travel from gut to brain through dura mater causing neurodegeneration. Reducing gut macrophages reduced protein spread and improved motor symptoms in mice. 50-90% of Parkinson's patients had gut symptoms decades before motor symptoms. The enteric nervous system (100-600M neurons) sends 90% of vagus nerve signals gut→brain, not brain→gut.
- **Eosteus chongqingensis (Nature, March 2026):** Oldest complete bony fish fossil, 436 million years old, 3cm long. From early Silurian deposits in Chongqing, China. Represents the most primitive bony fish before ray-finned/lobe-finned split. Ancestor of all bony fish and all tetrapods including humans.
- Lonsdaleite created in bulk — pure hexagonal diamond harder than cubic diamond.
- Brightest fast radio burst ever (RBFLOAT) — one year anniversary of detection.
