### US-Israel-Iran War — Day 13
- Death toll: 1,348+ civilians killed, 17,000+ injured since Feb 28.
- **Minab school strike:** US Tomahawk missile hit Shajareh Tayyebeh girls' school on Day 1 (Feb 28), killing 165-180, mostly children. Pentagon launched formal investigation — outdated DIA targeting data identified a former IRGC naval base; the school was walled off from the base between 2013-2016 but the coordinates were never updated. Multiple independent investigations (NYT, BBC Verify, NPR, CBC) confirm US responsibility.
- **Maritime escalation:** 6 ships struck by projectiles in Persian Gulf/Strait of Hormuz in 2 days. Two oil tankers hit near Iraq's Al Basrah port — first strikes in Iraqi waters. One Indian national killed. Iraq shut down oil port operations.
- **Selective Hormuz blockade:** Iran announced March 5 that the strait is closed only to US, Israeli, and Western allied ships. Iranian oil continues flowing to China (1.22M bpd, down from 2.16M pre-war). Chinese-linked vessels transiting under different security conditions.
- **IEA historic release:** 400 million barrels from strategic reserves (largest ever, dwarfing 2022's 182M during Ukraine war). US contributing 172M barrels starting next week. 120 days to fully deliver. Prices still above $100/barrel — release failed to drive down Brent crude.
- **Fertilizer crisis:** ~1/3 of global fertilizer trade transits Hormuz. ~50% of global urea and sulfur exports originate west of the strait. New Orleans urea prices jumped from $475 to $680/metric ton (+35%). Threatens spring planting globally. Sub-Saharan Africa, India, Bangladesh most vulnerable.
- Israel struck ~70 targets in Beirut area including IRGC Air Force HQ. Operations expanded into Lebanon.
- Iran fired advanced ballistic missiles at Tel Aviv and Haifa — "most intense operation since beginning of war."
- Kuwait, UAE, Saudi Arabia, Bahrain intercepting Iranian missiles and drones.
- **Iran withdraws from 2026 World Cup.** Sports minister: "Under no circumstances can we participate." First withdrawal from a World Cup due to active war with host country's coalition. FIFA seeking replacement.
- Iran's 3 conditions for peace: recognition of rights, reparations, guarantees against future aggression.
- US inflation held at 2.4% in February but oil surge threatens Fed's 2% target.
